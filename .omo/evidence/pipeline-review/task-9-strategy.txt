### 4. `TestPhysicallyReasonableOutputs` — 5 parametrized scenarios
Safety-net invariants applied to a 5-point sweep across the race:
**no negative fuel, no negative laps, no inf/nan, monotonic pit
window, tyre wear in [0,100], motor state in {1,2,3}**, and a full
recursive `_assert_finite()` walk of every float in the advice.

### Sanity check on physical reasonability

* **No negative fuel** — all `stint_end_fuel`, `fuel_needed_to_finish`,
  `one_less_stop_target_consumption` are non-negative.
* **No zero laps for non-finish** — `stint_end_laps` is always > 0
  when fuel > 0.
* **Pit window is monotonic**: `earliest <= optimal <= latest` in
  every scenario.
* **LATE race correctly identifies urgency**: `earliest_pit_lap = 78`
  is only 3 laps after `lap_number=75`, and `pit_stops_needed=1`
  proves the engine knows we can't finish on 12L of fuel.
* **HYBRID motor state correctly propagated**: explicit `motor_state=2`
  is reflected in `hybrid.inferred_motor_state=2`, and
  `fuel_energy_ratio` is positive and finite.

### One surprise (documented, NOT a bug)

`EARLY` scenario at lap 5 returns `pit_stops_needed=2` — not 0 or 1.
This is **correct** because: 75 laps remaining × 2.8 L/lap + 3 L
safety margin = 213 L total fuel needed, but we only have 95 L in
tank, so we must refuel 213 - 95 = 118 L of fuel over 100 L capacity
tanks = 1.18 stops, rounded up to **2**. The engine's "you need
exactly 1.18 stops" message is precisely the kind of physically
reasonable output the task brief asked us to verify.
